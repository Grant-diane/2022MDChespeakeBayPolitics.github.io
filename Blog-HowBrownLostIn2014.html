How Brown Lost in 2014 June 6th, 2022

Maryland has only had two Republican Governors in recent history, which is notable since Maryland is famously a very blue state. In 2014, Larry Hogan ran against Anthony Brown in the Maryland Governors Race. In the primaries the Democratic frontrunner was clearly Brown who was Lieutenant Governor of Maryland from 2007 to 2015 under former governor Martin O'Malley. Notably, Doug Gansler, another longstanding actor in the Maryland political scene, serving as Attorney General of Montgomery County and later for the state in 2006 and beyond. Gansler is running in the 2022 Gubernatorial election as well. Anthony Brown was highly qualified not only serving as lieutenant governor for so long, but as a lawyer and colonel in the U.S. Army reserves. Brown was the favorite in the race and many preliminary polls predicted that he would win easily against the Republican candidate. From all outsiders It seemed like the Maryland Governors Race was a sure bet for the democratic party. What they weren't betting on was the brilliant campaigning from the Republicans.

Hogan was much less established. Despite being the son of U.S. representative, Lawrence Hogan Sr. of maryland's fifth district, Hogan found only mild political success. Outside his time as State Secretary of Appointments, his experience largely comprised of losing in the Maryland 5th Congressional District race yet pulling much more of the vote than he should have with his experience and funding as measured up to his opponent. The frontrunner of the Republican primary was less clear, with Hogan polling just under 30% while his opponents David Craig and Charles Lollar were both polling a little over 10% in May-June of 2014.
